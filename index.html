Software Engineering Internship
Renovo Auto

java, android, adobe illustrator, 0MQ

A Renovo collaboration with their customer, the Stanford Dynamic Design Lab. Renovo supplied a high-power electric drivetrain to the research vehicle, a 1981 Delorean named MARTY.

I developed mobile applications that relayed telemetry data from the vehicle to the dashboard tablets, and to remote Android devices used to track vital vehicle data by Renovo engineers, such as battery temperatures and voltages.





I built the native applications using Java, and designed everything from scratch. I built all graphics elements in Adobe Illustrator. An Intel Edison was used to publish CAN data from an attached CAN card. I utilized the ZeroMQ Pub/Sub pattern to allow multipe Android devices to connect through a Wifi Direct connection.

Some early prototype designs.







And the final design showing most of the resulting data. A swipe on the lower screen reveals more detailed battery information.
